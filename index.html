Built on Reforestation

Wood is the most
sustainable building
material on earth.

Every Shoreline Design piece begins as a seed — planted in one of MasayaCo's reforestation projects on former cow pastures in Nicaragua. The wood grows, gets harvested responsibly, and is shaped by hand into furniture that will still be standing generations from now.

🌱

Seed to Seat

MasayaCo controls the entire supply chain — from their own forests to their 150,000 ft² production facility, to your door.

🌿

FSC-Certified Teak

All wood is sourced from FSC-certified reforestation projects. No old-growth forests. No shortcuts.

♻️

Carbon Captured in Every Piece

Wood is roughly 50% captured carbon. Every piece of furniture you own is a carbon store, not a carbon cost.

🔧

Designed to Be Repaired

Not designed to be replaced. Every piece can be rewoven, refinished, and restored — built for a lifetime, not a trend cycle.

Our Story

Started as a reforestation project. Became forever furniture.

MasayaCo was founded in 2008 by Aram Terry — a Peace Corps volunteer who fell in love with Nicaragua — and Abril Zepeda, a native of Masaya and master weaver. What began as an effort to reforest degraded cow pastures became one of Central America's most respected furniture companies.

Today, their 12-acre production facility employs 400 designers and artisans, operates its own sawmill and kilns, and ships to the world's finest hotels and residences. Shoreline Design brings this vision to Canada for the very first time.
